Business Continuity and Disaster Recovery

Reflections From This Unit

Summary

This unit took the risk analysis to another level by providing an overview of disaster recovery, business continuity, shared responsibility model, RTO and RPO. Disaster recovery allows businesses to recover from a failure, such as a loss of a local data centre, device, data, or connection link while allowing them to restore functionality, connectivity and data as quickly as possible following the failure. The shared responsibility model is a crucial component of understanding cloud security, where both the cloud provider such as Google, AWS or Microsoft, as well as the user are responsible for maintaining the principles of the CIA Triad. Generally speaking, the providers are responsible for the physical components, while the customer is responsible for maintaining the applications, software, data and security that they want to host on the platform. Of course, this depends on whether this is done as IaaS, PaaS or SaaS, with the providerâ€™s responsibility growing respectively. RTO is commonly used to measure the time the application needs to become available again, also sometimes covered under a Service Level Agreement (SLA), while RPO refers to the amount of data loss tolerated (mainly covered by durability), commonly covered in terms and conditions of the provider.
