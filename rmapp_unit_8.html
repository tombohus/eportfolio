Exercise 8.6C Solution

Notes:

"Based on the scenario provided, I would use an independent samples t-test to compare the mean income between males and females. The t-test is appropriate for comparing the means of two independent groups (males and females) and determining if there is a statistically significant difference between them (Kim, 2015). It aligns with the objective of testing whether male income exceeds female income."

"The results showed a statistically significant difference between the mean income for males (M=53.12) and females (M=44.42), , p=0.0016. The mean income for males exceeded that of females by $8.7.

Since the p-value is less than 0.05, there is sufficient evidence to reject the null hypothesis and conclude the population mean income for males is significantly higher than for females based on this sample.

The key assumptions for valid interpretation are:

• Income data is normally distributed in each group
• Variances of the income data are equal
• Observations are independent
• Income is a continuous numeric variable

• These were validated through histograms, statistical normality and variance tests prior to applying the t-test. The findings support the claim that average male income exceeds female income."
