Risk and Risk Management Process

Reflections From This Unit

Summary

During this week, there was a discussion about the common definitions of security and risk management. Many crucial components such as the CIA triad were discussed, which defines three primary components of security and their goals. Confidentiality guarantees, that only an authorised person can be granted access to the resource. This can be achieved by authentication, as well as encryption of the data, where unless the user has the necessary private key, no access can be provided. Integrity guarantees that the data has not been changed during transit. This can be achieved by generating hashes and then comparing the hashes after the transmission. Finally, availability was discussed, which guarantees that a resource can be accessed with minimal downtime. There are several other topics that could contribute to this component, such as disaster recovery, and architecting the system in a way, that a local failure should not affect the overall state of the system while minimizing the time it takes to reestablish connectivity. Standards such as OpenFAIR and OCTAVE were also discussed, which allow us to take a structured approach to characterize threats and minimise potential risks.
